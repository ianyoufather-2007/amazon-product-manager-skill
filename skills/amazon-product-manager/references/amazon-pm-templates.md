# Amazon Product Manager Templates

## PRD

Use this structure for a seller-facing or marketplace platform feature:

1. Title
2. Decision summary
3. Customer and user
4. Problem statement
5. Evidence
6. Goals and success metrics
7. Non-goals
8. Proposed solution
9. User stories
10. Requirements
11. Data and instrumentation
12. Launch plan
13. Risks and mitigations
14. Open questions

Minimum acceptance criteria:

- Requirements are testable.
- Metrics include baseline, target, and measurement window when available.
- Dependencies and owner handoffs are named.

## Opportunity Brief

Use this for product selection, category expansion, listing optimization, or ASIN strategy:

| Field | Content |
| --- | --- |
| Opportunity | One-sentence bet |
| Marketplace | Amazon marketplace and category |
| Demand signal | Keyword, rank, trend, review, or search evidence |
| Competitive gap | Price, content, reviews, fulfillment, features, or brand gap |
| Economics | Price, COGS, FBA/referral fees, ads, margin, return risk |
| Execution path | Listing, sourcing, launch, ads, inventory, review plan |
| Decision | Go, test, wait, or reject |

## Market Screening Memo

Use this before product selection when the user is choosing a category, market, niche, scenario, or product line.

| Field | Content |
| --- | --- |
| Market decision | Enter Market, Watch Market, or Avoid Market |
| Market arena | Broad demand space, marketplace, and category |
| Scenario cluster | Specific use cases, buyer segments, and purchase occasions |
| Market thesis | Why this market could become a product line, not just one SKU |
| Keyword depth | Core terms, long-tail clusters, modifiers, and buyer intent |
| Price-band health | Low/mid/premium bands, visible value drivers, race-to-bottom risk |
| Competitive structure | Head brands, review barriers, listing quality, brand concentration |
| Small-brand wedge | First SKU or bundle that can enter without large-brand resources |
| Economics | Target price band, estimated margin room, FBA fit, ad tolerance |
| Operations | Supplier feasibility, MOQ, lead time, compliance, QA, support risk |
| Portfolio path | Second and third SKU if the first wedge works |
| Validation plan | 7-day research plan or 30-day test plan |

## Market Portfolio Map

Use this when comparing multiple categories, niches, scenarios, or product lines.

| Market | Decision | Demand | Competition | Margin | Small-brand fit | Portfolio path | Main risk | Next action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Market A | Enter / Watch / Avoid | 1-5 | 1-5 | 1-5 | 1-5 | 1-5 | Risk | Action |

Rank finalists by:

- Market score and evidence quality
- Small-brand execution fit
- Validation speed and capital required
- Ability to build a product line rather than a one-off SKU
- Strategic fit with existing supply chain, audience, content, or brand promise

## Prioritization

Score each option from 1 to 5:

| Factor | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Customer impact | How much the buyer or seller pain is reduced |
| Business impact | Revenue, margin, retention, efficiency, or risk reduction |
| Confidence | Quality of evidence behind the bet |
| Effort | Engineering, operations, supply, creative, or compliance cost |
| Time criticality | Seasonality, competitor movement, or launch window |

Default recommendation formula:

`priority = (customer impact + business impact + confidence + time criticality) / effort`

Do not let the score replace judgment. Call out strategic overrides.

## Roadmap

Use Now / Next / Later unless the user asks for calendar quarters.

| Horizon | Theme | Initiative | Why now | Success metric | Dependencies |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Now | 0-6 weeks | Highest confidence execution | Existing evidence or deadline | Metric and target | Named blockers |
| Next | 6-12 weeks | Validated follow-on | Needs learning or prep | Metric and target | Named blockers |
| Later | 12+ weeks | Strategic option | Lower certainty or larger scope | Metric and target | Named blockers |

## Product Selection Memo

Use this when the user asks whether a product, keyword, niche, or category is worth entering.

| Field | Content |
| --- | --- |
| Decision | Go, No-Go, Test First, or Rework Offer |
| Product / niche | Product name, keyword, category, and marketplace |
| Target customer | Buyer segment, use case, and purchase trigger |
| Demand evidence | Keywords, search intent, trend, BSR/rank proxy, or review demand |
| Competition evidence | Top competitors, price band, rating/review barriers, listing quality, brand concentration |

## Listing Optimization Brief

Use this for ASIN/listing repair, conversion improvement, keyword coverage, or competitor content gaps.

1. Decision summary: keep, rework, test, or reposition.
2. Current bottleneck: CTR, CVR, keyword rank, rating, reviews, price, or traffic quality.
3. Evidence: competitor comparison, review/Q&A pain points, keyword gaps, image/content gaps, and performance metrics.
4. Recommended changes:
   - Title and keyword coverage
   - Main image and secondary images
   - Bullets and A+ content
   - Offer, price, coupon, bundle, or variation structure
   - Review/Q&A and compliance fixes
5. Experiment plan: treatment, control, primary metric, guardrails, duration, and rollback trigger.
6. Expected impact: CTR, CVR, rank, return rate, or margin.

## PPC Diagnosis Plan

Use this when ads are inefficient, launch traffic is weak, ACoS/TACoS is high, or keyword rank is not moving.

| Area | Questions |
| --- | --- |
| Objective | Launch ranking, profitability, harvesting, defense, or liquidation? |
| Structure | Auto, broad, phrase, exact, product targeting, brand defense, competitor targeting |
| Search terms | Winners, spend drains, irrelevant terms, negative keyword needs |
| Economics | Break-even ACoS, current ACoS/TACoS, CPC, CVR, contribution margin |
| Funnel | CTR problem, CVR problem, price problem, listing problem, review problem |
| Action | Cut, isolate, bid down, bid up, negate, harvest, test creative/listing, or stop |

End with a 7-14 day action plan and a decision rule.

## Profit Rescue Plan

Use this when revenue exists but true profit is weak or unclear.

1. Profit waterfall: price, COGS, inbound freight, FBA fee, referral fee, storage, coupon, ads, returns, removals, net profit.
2. Break-even points: minimum price, maximum COGS, maximum ACoS, maximum return rate.
3. Root cause: price too low, COGS too high, FBA tier issue, ads inefficient, returns high, coupon overuse, or inventory drag.
4. Rescue levers ranked by dollar impact:
   - Raise price or change coupon
   - Reduce COGS/freight
   - Improve FBA size/packaging
   - Reduce wasted PPC
   - Improve conversion
   - Reduce returns
   - Liquidate aging inventory
5. Decision: scale, stabilize, rework, or exit.

## Inventory Risk Review

Use this for stockout, overstock, aging inventory, reorder, or liquidation decisions.

| Field | Content |
| --- | --- |
| Decision | Reorder, hold, reduce, liquidate, or kill |
| Current inventory | Units, days of cover, aged inventory, inbound units |
| Demand pace | Daily/weekly sales, seasonality, launch/growth stage |
| Supply constraints | Lead time, MOQ, supplier reliability, defect risk |
| Cash risk | Capital tied, storage fees, markdown cost, opportunity cost |
| Trigger | Stockout date, reorder point, liquidation threshold, review date |

## Weekly Amazon Business Review

Use this for operating reviews, boss updates, weekly reports, or executive summaries.

1. Headline decision: what changed and what needs action.
2. Scorecard: sales, units, sessions, CVR, CTR, ACoS, TACoS, organic rank, rating, reviews, returns, net margin, inventory cover.
3. What worked: actions that moved metrics.
4. What broke: bottlenecks, risks, or surprises.
5. Product decisions: continue, scale, rework, pause, liquidate, or investigate.
6. Next week actions: owner, metric, target, due date.
7. Open asks: budget, inventory, creative, sourcing, compliance, or leadership decision.

## Kill / Continue Decision Memo

Use this when the product may need to be stopped, liquidated, relaunched, or heavily reworked.

| Field | Content |
| --- | --- |
| Recommendation | Continue, rework, pause, liquidate, or kill |
| Current state | Sales, margin, ads, inventory, rating, returns, rank |
| Fixability | What can be fixed versus structural blockers |
| Recovery plan | Actions, cost, time, expected upside |
| Exit plan | Markdown, liquidation, ad shutdown, inventory removal, supplier stop |
| Decision trigger | Metric or date that forces the final call |
